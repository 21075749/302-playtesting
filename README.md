## Iteration Notes (Post-Playtest)
Playtesting Summary
During playtesting, the player generally found the rhythm game concept straightforward and engaging, but experienced some difficulty adjusting to the timing mechanics of the hit bar. The player asked whether they should press the key before or after the block reaches the line, indicating initial uncertainty about the correct timing window. They also mentioned that the hit bar felt too strict, making it difficult to hit precisely and forcing them to sometimes spam keys to land a successful hit.
Another issue observed was that mistakes reset the sequence too quickly, which prevented the player from understanding what they did wrong. The player suggested that additional feedback or a clearer indication of timing errors could help improve learning.
Despite these challenges, the player remained motivated rather than frustrated, suggesting the core gameplay loop is engaging. The player also responded positively to the visual design and background graphics, noting that the game reminded them of piano-tile style rhythm games.
The player also proposed several ideas that could increase engagement, including animations, combo systems, score tracking, power-ups, and new mechanics introduced across levels.
Three Changes to Implement Before the Mid-Term Showcase

1. Improve Timing Feedback
The player struggled to understand the correct timing for pressing keys. To address this:
> Add visual tap feedback when a key is pressed (flash, glow, or particle effect).
> Show whether the input was “perfect,” “early,” or “late.”
> This will help players learn the timing window more quickly and reduce the need to spam keys.

2. Adjust the Hit Window and Error Feedback
The hit bar currently feels too strict and mistakes reset too quickly.
Changes:
> Slightly increase the hit window tolerance.
> Add a brief delay or visual cue when a mistake occurs so players can recognize what went wrong before restarting.
> This will make the game feel fairer and easier to learn.

3. Add Progression Elements to Increase Engagement
The player suggested features that could make gameplay more rewarding.
Possible additions:
> Score system or progress bar
> Combo multiplier for consecutive successful hits
> Gradually introduce new mechanics each level (e.g., more keys, obstacles, tremor effects)
> These elements will create clear progression and motivation for players to improve.
